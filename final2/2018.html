STUDIO FOUNDATIONS 1

FALL SEMESTER

Primary and Secondary Color Scale

While creating the “Primary and Secondary Color Scales,”I had also created two separate color scales, exploiting each of primary and secondary colors. Using acrylic paint and starting with the primary colors I mixed each of the three with one another and constructed the secondary colors. My second scale analyzed the hues of these colors. Using a variety or grays, I found different temperatures within each color. Lastly, My color wheel displays the primary and secondary colors and a variety in their intensity.

Tint, Tone, & Shade Color Scale

Monochromatic Color Scheme (still life painting)

“Blossom,” my still life painting was painted from the perspective of a blossoming flower in a vase. There were two different sources of back/left side light which created value and definition thought my piece. Using only the hue of red, I was able to create the space of illusion and emotion through expression. The dominant color red, when mixed portray different values, creates many pink tones along with rusty purple. This colors typically represent love and affection was flowers express these emotions.

Complimentary Color Scheme (leaf painting)

“Drift,” my Leaf painting was inspired by the autumn leaves and unique colors and diversities they offer. I loved working with the light primaries and their additive mixtures. The blue background contradicts the warmer colors in the leaves while showing unity with green tones.

Color Linoleum Block Print

These prints were made based off the idea of a locket and the female figure. The oval-shape symbolizes the the shape of the necklace, the color scheme is very feminine, along with a female figure. This technique of art and the concept behind it really ties together. Lockets signify the olden day woman and reflect a bit of the woman in that time.

SPRING SEMESTER

Living In the Past

ARTIST STATEMENT

Identity is an individual concept and is singular to oneself. The past is a large part of my identity and representing myself through past experiences highlights my inner child. I have always categorized myself as different then others because of the way I have portrayed myself and my distinction between they way others see me and I see myself. Through the work of photoshop I was able to place myself into older outdated photos, and in doing so the idea of time reference is displayed. Capturing myself in these old fashioned photos contrasts my past through the view of a historical past and contributing to the awareness of who am I.

PROJECT STATEMENT

When a viewers is looking at my work I want there to be a clear distinction of the past. Based on my up brings Id hope to make a clear point in where I identify childhood and the ways my character stands out in relation to others. By placing myself, as a child, in photos before my time it emphasizes the idea of time relativity and symbolizing the time frame in which I associate my self in. When looking at these photos, I want my views to see a point in my life that reflects a part of who I am but makes the connection that I am not longer this person.

MATERIALS

The materials I will need for this project will be Adobe Photoshop, along with photo paper and magnets to hang my work. I will also use a string to surround my photos creating a large boarder.

Clay Project - Coral

ARTIST STATEMENT

Through the use of clay, the forms I have created are meant to loosely resemble coral. The variety of size and shape creates the structure of the lifeforms growing under the water. When looking at this artwork I want the viewer to take away the sense of community and unity thought the work. The funky shapes are meant to play with the mind and allow the viewer to play with the idea of abstract structures.

PROJECT STATEMENT

This project was influenced from my love and curiosity of the ocean and what is living below the waters. I choose to create several structures that were ideally similar but also contrasted in form. The eye is drawn to colors of these objects displaying the liveliness while the multitude of lays portrayed the naturalist sense of the objects.

MATERIALS & SCALE

The materials I will need to complete this project are clay, clay extruder, kiln, and acrylic paint.

The scale of my overall project will fit in a 9in by 9in perimeter while each piece will vary in size, but all around the size of 4in by 3in by 3in.

Metal Work Project

ARTIST STATEMENT

Through the use of metal, I have created a stair way which leads to confusion. The form of a traditional stair case is meant to lead you somewhere, where as in my work, the objective is to draw your attention to the abstract matter of where are you going. The movement displayed creates an unrealistic representation as to our outcomes and leaves us with the idea that our subjectivity is what we are following. The basic structure that is holding the piece together is the also the structure of the stairs itself. The idea that the stairs are the first motion to moving forward creates a form of stability but as your eyes move up and around the object the lines blur between where the stairs stop. The curved and twisted metal pieces can be representations of obstacles that one must face while also relating to the possibilities moving forward.

PROJECT STATEMENT

This project was inspired by curiosity of where am I going next and what will take me there. Through the configuration of the object an understanding of direction is created while also the movement plays with the amount of different paths that can be taken.Taking away for “Visual Stair,” I want the viewer to create their own understanding of where their mind will lead them rather than just following the steps.

MATERIALS & SCALE

The materials I will need for this project will be sheet metal, along with welding tools such as Metal Brake, Metal Roller, Metal Shear, and Spot Welder.

The size of “Visual Stair” will be approximately 1.5, by 1.5, by 1.5 feet.

Time Arts Project

ARTIST STATEMENT

Art exists in time and space, implying change and movement, which then implies the passage of time. Through the experience of my video, I want my viewers to explore movement through human interaction and evaluate the collective movement created in groups of people. With the blurred video effect added to each clip allows the viewer to separate their personal connections and draw their attention to the raw act of movement in a short period of time. Each clip only lasting about ten seconds displays the amount of movement that is produced around us in that brief moment. While the sound to the video doesn’t involve much content, the sound is slowed version of background noise which is to allow the viewer to slow down and examine their interpretations of subtle change in the environment around them.

PROJECT STATEMENT

Through the use of adobe Premiere, my video takes the viewer on a first person perspective journey where movement is observed and explored. From walking around severely different areas, with a variety of populations and contrast in movement, a form of self awareness is created. Viewing human movement in a realistic environment allows for a deeper understanding of what happens to move around one and they are progressing throughout there day. In the smallest amount of time, more movement is produced but going unnoticeably while many may not realize what is going on around them.

MATERIALS

Adobe Premiere, Camera/Video Recorder, populated environments
